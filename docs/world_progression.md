# World Progression Design

## Core Philosophy

The world progression is **not** based on map level.

Instead:

- **Map = Biome / Ecosystem**
- **Tier = Difficulty**

Players choose a **biome** because they want its monsters, materials, bosses, scenery, or unique mechanics—not because it has a higher level range.

---

# Tier System

Every map supports every Tier.

Example:

Green Plains

- Tier 1
- Tier 2
- Tier 3
...
- Tier 10

Misty Swamp

- Tier 1
- Tier 2
- Tier 3
...
- Tier 10

Snow Mountain

- Tier 1
- Tier 2
- Tier 3
...
- Tier 10

Tier determines:

- Monster Level
- Monster Stats
- Monster AI
- Monster Skills
- Drop Quality
- Experience
- Boss Difficulty
- Reward Quality

Maps never become obsolete.

---

# Monster Evolution

All monster species exist across every Tier.

Monsters are **not replaced** by higher-level versions.

Instead, every species evolves as Tier increases.

Example:

Wolf

- Tier 1 — Young Wolf
- Tier 2 — Adult Wolf
- Tier 3 — Elite Wolf
- Tier 4 — Giant Wolf
- Tier 5 — Corrupted Wolf
- Tier 6+ — Alpha / Ancient Variants

Evolution includes:

- Larger body size
- Visual upgrades
- Additional skills
- Improved AI
- Better drops

Avoid making monsters feel like simple HP inflation.
Every Tier should make the creature feel naturally stronger.

---

# Map Identity

Maps are differentiated by ecosystem rather than level.

Example:

Green Plains

- Wolves
- Boars
- Cows
- Wood Elementals

Misty Swamp

- Poison Frogs
- Swamp Snakes
- Lizards
- Water Elementals

Snow Mountain

- Snow Wolves
- Ice Bears
- Ice Spirits
- Ice Elementals

Players visit maps because they want their unique ecosystem.

---

# Exploration Motivation

Maps should provide exclusive content.

Examples:

- Exclusive monsters
- Exclusive pets
- Exclusive materials
- Exclusive bosses
- Exclusive gemstones
- Exclusive crafting resources
- Exclusive events
- Unique environmental mechanics

Example:

Snow Mountain

- Blizzard
- Frozen lakes
- Slippery terrain

Misty Swamp

- Poison mist
- Mud slowing
- Toxic pools

Maps should feel mechanically different, not just visually different.

---

# Tier Unlock Progression

Higher Tiers are unlocked progressively.

Example:

Green Plains

- Tier 1 ✅
- Tier 2 ✅
- Tier 3 ✅
- Tier 4 🔒
- Tier 5 🔒

Unlock conditions may include:

- Defeat previous Tier Boss
- Complete exploration objectives
- Finish story quests
- Reach account progression milestones

Players should gradually unlock harder versions of every biome.

---

# Benefits

- No obsolete maps.
- Every biome remains valuable forever.
- Players can level anywhere they enjoy.
- Friends can play together by selecting the same map and an appropriate Tier.
- Art assets have long-term value.
- Monster collection remains meaningful.
- World exploration is driven by ecosystem instead of level requirements.

---

# Design Principle

Do not design maps with fixed level ranges.

❌ Traditional RPG

- Green Plains: Lv 1–20
- Swamp: Lv 20–40
- Snow Mountain: Lv 40–60

✅ This Game

Every biome supports every Tier.

Map determines:

- Ecosystem
- Monsters
- Resources
- Bosses
- Environment

Tier determines:

- Difficulty
- Monster evolution
- Rewards
- AI
- Progression

The player's journey is driven by discovering new ecosystems, collecting unique monsters and resources, and overcoming increasingly difficult Tiers—not by abandoning old maps for higher-level zones.
